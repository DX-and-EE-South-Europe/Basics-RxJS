Observables can be infinite or finite. When they are finite, it means that the observables complete and stop execution. However, the infinite observables never complete, so it could be a waste of computation power or memory resources when we no longer need them. For this reason, the unsubscribe method of the Subscription is used.

When you subscribe to an observable, it get a Subscription that represents the current execution of the observable and its mainly used to cancel the execution. To cancel the execution, you must call the unsubscribe method from the Subscription, it will execute the return of the observable.

Note: Unsubscribing does not mean that the observable is completed. It means that you do not listen the emissions of the Observable yet, but the observable still emitting values. Therefore, it is really important that when unsubscribing, the complete method of the observable is called internally (in the return of the observable).
